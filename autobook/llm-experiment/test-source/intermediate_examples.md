> **Pedagogical notes:**
> - **No. 5 & 11:** The separate "Discount on bonds payable" and "Discount on long-term payables" accounts are shown to illustrate the discount amortization concept. In practice under IFRS, financial liabilities are recorded at their initial proceeds and carried at amortized cost — no separate discount line appears on the balance sheet.
> - **No. 25:** Purchase price allocated by relative fair value — Land: $9,000,000 × ($8,000,000 / $12,000,000) = $6,000,000; Building: $9,000,000 × ($4,000,000 / $12,000,000) = $3,000,000. VAT of $900,000 (10% × $9,000,000) is recoverable and recorded as VAT receivable. Cash paid = $4,500,000 + $900,000 sales tax = $5,400,000.
> - **No. 26:** Payroll deduction categories (pension, health insurance, employment insurance, income tax) are generalized IFRS labels. For Canadian localization: CPP, EI, provincial health premiums, federal/provincial income tax. For US localization: Social Security, Medicare (FICA), state unemployment, federal/state income tax.
> - **No. 31:** Sales tax is non-recoverable (vehicle used partly for personal commuting), so it is capitalized into the asset cost per IAS 16.
