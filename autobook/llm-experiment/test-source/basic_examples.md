# Basic Level — Accounting Transaction Examples (IFRS)

| No. | Company | Transaction Description | Debit Nature | Credit Nature | Debit | Credit | IFRS Ref |
|-----|---------|------------------------|-------------|--------------|-------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Maple Goods | Maple Goods purchased merchandise inventory and paid $100 in cash. | Increase in assets | Decrease in assets | {Inventories — Merchandise: $100} | {Cash: $100} | IAS 2 |
| 2 | Maple Goods | Maple Goods purchased merchandise inventory of $300 on account. | Increase in assets | Increase in liabilities | {Inventories — Merchandise: $300} | {Trade payables: $300} | IAS 2 |
| 3 | Maple Goods | Maple Goods issued 200 common shares (par value $5.00 per share) at $9.00 per share. Total proceeds of $1,800 were deposited into the company's bank account. | Increase in assets | Increase in equity | {Cash: $1,800} | {Share capital — Common: $1,000, Additional paid-in capital: $800} | IAS 32 |
| 4 | Maple Goods | Maple Goods sold merchandise (cost $300) for $500 in cash. | {Increase in assets, Expense recognition} | {Revenue recognition, Decrease in assets} | {Cash: $500, Cost of goods sold: $300} | {Revenue — Product sales: $500, Inventories — Merchandise: $300} | IFRS 15 / IAS 2 |
| 5 | Maple Goods | Maple Goods settled accounts payable of $50 by issuing a demand cheque. | Decrease in liabilities | Decrease in assets | {Trade payables: $50} | {Cash: $50} | — |
| 6 | Maple Goods | Maple Goods repaid an existing bank loan of $100 and immediately refinanced at a lower interest rate. | {Decrease in liabilities} | {Increase in liabilities} | {Notes payable: $100} | {Notes payable: $100} | IFRS 9 |
| 7 | Maple Goods | Maple Goods converted a bank loan of $200 into equity by issuing 400 common shares (par value $5.00 per share) at par. | Decrease in liabilities | Increase in equity | {Notes payable: $200} | {Share capital — Common: $200} | IAS 32 |
| 8 | Maple Goods | Maple Goods delivered merchandise to a customer against a previously received customer deposit of $100. | Decrease in liabilities | Revenue recognition | {Contract liabilities: $100} | {Revenue — Product sales: $100} | IFRS 15 |
| 9 | Maple Goods | Maple Goods repurchased 100 of its own common shares from existing shareholders for $200 in cash. | Decrease in equity | Decrease in assets | {Treasury shares: $200} | {Cash: $200} | IAS 32 |
| 10 | Maple Goods | The board of directors declared a common share cash dividend of $50, payable in 30 days. | Decrease in equity | Increase in liabilities | {Retained earnings: $50} | {Dividends payable: $50} | IAS 32 |
| 11 | Maple Goods | Maple Goods converted 50 preferred shares ($100 carrying value) into 25 common shares. | Decrease in equity | Increase in equity | {Share capital — Preferred: $100} | {Share capital — Common: $100} | IAS 32 |
| 12 | Maple Goods | Maple Goods paid employee salaries of $100 in cash. | Expense recognition | Decrease in assets | {Salaries expense: $100} | {Cash: $100} | IAS 19 |
| 13 | Maple Goods | Maple Goods accrued an unpaid utility bill of $50. | Expense recognition | Increase in liabilities | {Utilities expense: $50} | {Accrued liabilities: $50} | — |
| 14 | Maple Goods | Merchandise with a carrying value of $2,000 was completely destroyed by a typhoon. | Expense recognition | Decrease in assets | {Casualty loss: $2,000} | {Inventories — Merchandise: $2,000} | IAS 37 |
| 15 | Maple Goods | The board of directors resolved to issue new shares to fund a factory production line expansion. | — | — | No journal entry required. This is a board resolution — a memorandum item only. No assets, liabilities, or equity have changed. A journal entry is recorded only when shares are actually issued and proceeds received. | — | — |

> **Pedagogical notes:**
> - **No. 6:** The refinancing is recorded as a single net entry — the old obligation is extinguished and replaced by the new one at a lower rate. In practice, if cash flows between counterparties, two separate entries may be warranted. This example assumes a direct modification with no cash movement.
> - **No. 7:** Assumes par value ($5.00 × 400 shares = $200) equals the carrying value of the loan, so no additional paid-in capital arises. If the shares were issued above par, the excess would be credited to additional paid-in capital.
> - **No. 8:** Revenue is recognized at the point of delivery when the performance obligation under IFRS 15 is satisfied. The corresponding cost of goods sold entry is omitted here as the cost of the delivered merchandise is not stated in the transaction.
> - **No. 11:** Recorded using the book value method under IAS 32. If the par values of preferred and common shares differ, the difference would be allocated to additional paid-in capital or retained earnings as appropriate.
> - **No. 14:** Casualty losses from natural disasters are recognized as losses in profit or loss under IAS 37. They are not classified as extraordinary items under IFRS (no such category exists).
> - **No. 15:** Board resolutions, authorizations, and commitments do not meet the definition of a transaction under IFRS — no economic event has occurred. These are memorandum items disclosed in the notes to the financial statements, not posted to the general ledger.
